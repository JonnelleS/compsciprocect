Reading

Reading is very important for future jobs and living in general and it is also a fun activity to pass time. You'll have to understand grammar and sentence structure for writing essays and understanding different books and stories as you grow older. When reading you gain an understanding of when to use words in a sentence and boost your vocabulary. If you read more and more you can improve writing and memory skills.


You can get books at a local library or within your school. It is important to have a quiet place when you read so that you don't get distracted. So find your favourite place to read, pick up your favorite books, and read as often as you can!!

Grammar

Here are some tips for reading and writing:

A noun names a person, animal, place, or thing. And is singular, plural, or possessive.
A verb shows action and can be a main verb or a helping verb, like "were" or "has." Verbs also indicate tense and sometimes change their form to show past, present, or future tense."
An adjective describes and modifies nouns by adding descriptors such as size, shape, color.
An adverb will modify a verb and tell more about it, like how much, when, where, why, or how.

Other important grammatical rules are:

  • Capitalization of the first letter of the first word in a sentence and the title of books.
  • The rule "i" before "e" except after "c" is very common. For example "ceiled" and "perceived" are "cei" but "believe" and "piece" all follow the rule. There arewords that break the rule like "height".
  • Always ennd a sentence with a period(.) or pause a sentence with a comma(,).
  • Know when to write in past, future, or present tense and when not to use "I" in a paper.
